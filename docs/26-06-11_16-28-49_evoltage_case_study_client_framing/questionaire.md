# Questionnaire — Evoltage Case Study Client Framing

## Q1: Who is the specific person you imagine reading this case study on kicksnare.digital?
*e.g., a plumber deciding whether to get a new website, a small e-commerce owner comparing agencies, a marketing manager at a mid-size company?*

**Assumed answer (from wiki context):** A local trades business owner (electrician, plumber, builder, roofer) or small local services business owner in the UK, aged 30-55, who knows their website is bad but hasn't done anything about it. Budget-conscious (£500-2,000 range). Not technically sophisticated — they don't know what "schema markup" or "CRO" means. They found kicksnare.digital through cold outreach, a social media post, or a referral.

## Q2: What is the single most important action you want them to take after reading the case study?
*e.g., DM on X, fill out a contact form, book a call, forward it to someone?*

**Assumed answer:** DM @kicksnare on X, email rj@kicksnare.studio, or request the free audit — any first-contact action. The CTA section already has these.

## Q3: What do you think the biggest objection or anxiety is in a potential client's mind when they land on this case study?
*e.g., "This will be expensive," "I don't understand what they do," "This looks too good to be true," "They haven't done this for anyone real yet"?*

**Assumed answer:** "This looks impressive but they haven't actually done this for a paying client yet — it's just a mock." Secondary: "I don't understand half of what they're talking about." Tertiary: "How much does this cost?"

## Q4: How important is it that the case study also impresses other agencies, investors, or sophisticated buyers — not just small business owners?
*i.e., is this a single-audience piece, or does it need to serve multiple audiences?*

**Assumed answer:** Primary audience is small business owners (lead generation). Secondary audience is peers/agencies who might refer clients or validate credibility on social media. The current framing over-serves the secondary audience at the expense of the primary.

## Q5: If you had to pick one sentence a potential client should walk away thinking, what would it be?
*e.g., "These people really understand my problem," "They can do this fast and affordably," "My website is costing me money and they can fix it"?*

**Assumed answer:** "My website is costing me customers and these people can fix it in 3 days for £500."
