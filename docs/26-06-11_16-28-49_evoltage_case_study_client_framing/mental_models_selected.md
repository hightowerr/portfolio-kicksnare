# Mental Model Selection

## Selection Funnel

From an initial scan of ~280 mental model files across 17 categories, 6 candidates were deeply evaluated:

| Candidate | Category | Verdict |
|-----------|----------|---------|
| Curse of Knowledge | Human Nature | **SELECTED (#2)** |
| Jobs To Be Done | Strategic Thinking | **SELECTED (#1)** |
| Aristotle's Rhetorical Triangle | Human Nature | **SELECTED (#3)** |
| Marketing Myopia | Economics | Rejected — overlaps with JTBD; same core insight (selling methodology vs. client outcome) |
| Challenge-Action-Result (CAR) | Information | Rejected — useful formatting tool but too tactical for this diagnostic |
| Audience | Art | Rejected — relevant but generic; Curse of Knowledge is the sharper, more diagnostic version |

## Final Ranked Selection

### 1. Jobs To Be Done (JTBD)
**Rationale:** The case study is a product that potential clients "hire" to do a job. The current template assumes the job is "show me your diagnostic methodology" — but the real job is "give me confidence that this agency can get me more customers, fast, for a fair price." JTBD's four-forces framework (push/pull/anxiety/inertia) maps directly to the client's decision to hire an agency. Its "Keywords for Situations" include "'inferior' competitor winning via simplicity" — which is exactly what a simpler, outcome-focused case study would do against Kicksnare's methodology-heavy version.

**Role in the analysis:** Reframes WHAT the case study should say by identifying the job the client is actually hiring it to do.

### 2. Curse of Knowledge
**Rationale:** The user has deep expertise in CRO methodology, Nielsen heuristics, Speero frameworks, ICE scoring, and Friction-Reward ratios. This expertise is visible in the case study drafts — terms like "FR ratio 1.80", "LocalBusiness JSON-LD", "compose-first Tailwind v4" — all of which are invisible to the target audience (a local tradesperson). The model's "Keywords for Situations" explicitly list "analytics presentations with unexplained stats" and "clinical/technical jargon" — both present in the current framing. The Thinking Step "Show, don't tour your analysis" is the single most actionable instruction for fixing the case study.

**Role in the analysis:** Diagnoses WHY the current framing is wrong — the user literally cannot see the case study through a non-expert's eyes without deliberate debiasing.

### 3. Aristotle's Rhetorical Triangle
**Rationale:** The current case study over-indexes on logos (methodology, data, frameworks, technical metrics) and under-indexes on ethos (credibility, trust — especially without a real client testimonial) and pathos (emotional resonance — "your phone isn't ringing because your website is broken"). The model prescribes how to rebalance the three appeals for the target audience. Its thinking steps — "Define the goal", "Map the audience", "Balance the three appeals", "Test alignment" — provide a structural framework for rewriting.

**Role in the analysis:** Prescribes HOW to restructure the message — which appeals to strengthen, which to reduce, and how to rebalance for a non-expert audience.

## How the Three Models Interlock

- **JTBD** answers: "What does the client need this case study to do for them?"
- **Curse of Knowledge** answers: "Why is the current version failing to do that?"
- **Rhetorical Triangle** answers: "How should the case study be restructured to succeed?"

Together they form a complete diagnostic-to-prescription chain: identify the job → diagnose the communication failure → prescribe the structural fix.
